NEGATIVE IMPACTS OF TECHNOLOGY ON WATCH INDUSTRY

The 21st century, often referred to as the age of information and technology, has completely transformed the world. The incorporation of technology has simplified complex tasks, allowing them to be approached in a more efficient manner. Recently, the watch industry has been significantly influenced by advancements and the implementation of modern technology. This has resulted in both beneficial and detrimental effects on the sector. We have personally undertaken some research regarding these effects.

However, these changes do not always yield positive results in the watch sector. There are negative consequences that have led to the erosion of identity and a decline in the popularity of traditional watches. The advancement of smartwatches has resulted in a reduction in the variety of watch designs. While it’s not that all designs are identical, there is a noticeable similarity among them, making it challenging to identify different watch brands. The watch industry, once celebrated for its unique and distinctive designs, has seen these innovations become a rarity. Traditional classic watches, known for their timeless elegance, are now less favoured by younger consumers, leading to a significant decrease in sales. If this trend continues, the production of such watches may cease altogether. Additionally, the amount of electronic waste generated during manufacturing and from consumer use over the years could result in increased environmental concerns.

In conclusion, like every coin with two sides, the application of technology in the watch industry brings both positive and negative effects. Change can be beneficial for a period, but in the long term, it may create lasting impacts that are difficult to rectify. While the technological features offer numerous advantages, we must consider the extent of their implications.
